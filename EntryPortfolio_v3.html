OverviewHow the Complete Entry System Works

Two Paths Into One Conversation

Every entry — human-led or product-led — has one destination: the Capital Exposure Diagnostic.

Some executives will be brought into the capital exposure conversation through a relationship. Others will find their way independently — through a talk, an article, a peer referral, or a tool that produces a finding they didn't expect. This system provides both paths.

Human-Led · S01–S06
Service Entries
Six facilitated engagements matched to buyer type and emotional state. Each requires a relationship context. Each produces a real finding through structured conversation or workshop.
  • Requires facilitator presence
  • Finding co-constructed with buyer
  • Priced from complimentary to $25K
  • Entry trigger: relationship or referral
  • Converts: session to Diagnostic mandate
Product-Led · P01–P10
Product Entries
Ten self-directed instruments and tools. No relationship context required. Each produces a finding the executive generates themselves — harder to dismiss than a finding a vendor delivers.
  • No facilitator required for most
  • Finding self-generated by executive
  • All free or lightly gated for user
  • Entry trigger: thought leadership, search, cold contact
  • Converts: self-discomfort to conversation request

The governing test for every entry is identical: does completing this leave the executive with a finding they want to resolve?

A capability demonstration produces admiration. A finding produces demand. The output is what converts — not the experience, not the relationship, not the pitch.

The four entries marked ◆ are architecturally non-obvious. They reach buyers the other entries do not — the skeptic, the constrained CEO, the career-focused individual, and the Think Wrong client with no path to the governance conversation.

S01Service EntryPrimary Buyer: Senior Executive — Warm Relationship

Capital Exposure Briefing

"Let us show you what we see in your situation."

Entry Context
Warm relationship, senior executive who knows your work and is curious — but has no active mandate and no budget committed. They've heard the ideas. They're not yet activated. The goal is to create a finding they want to resolve.
Format & Duration
A prepared 60–90 minute facilitated conversation. You arrive with a one-page Preliminary Exposure Hypothesis built from publicly available signals — their industry, AI investment patterns, public growth statements, and analyst commentary.
Deliverable
A written two-page Preliminary Exposure Hypothesis left with the executive. Three exposure zones identified. One or two questions framed that they likely cannot answer with current internal visibility.
What It Costs Them
Zero budget required. 90 minutes of executive time. No internal data access needed. Price for SolveNext: complimentary — value is pipeline activation, not revenue.
Why It Works

The power of this offer is in what you bring before you walk in the room. A written hypothesis based on external signals signals three things simultaneously: that you've done serious work, that you see things others don't, and that there's a gap between their public posture and their likely internal reality.

The written hypothesis is the psychological mechanism. It creates a documented gap — something named and specific — that they now want to close. Curiosity converts to urgency when the problem has a name and a form.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"This is based on external signals only. We can only see so far from the outside. The Diagnostic gives you the real picture — the actual capital exposure architecture across creation, protection, and liberation. What we've shown you today is the hypothesis. The Diagnostic is the answer."

S02Service EntryPrimary Buyer: CEO / Chief Strategy Officer / CINO

Portfolio Imbalance Session

"Let's look at where your capital is actually allocated."

Entry Context
Strategy or innovation relationship. They sense growth is underperforming but can't name why. Often preceded by "we're investing but not seeing returns." The problem is felt but unlocated.
Format & Duration
Half-day facilitated working session with 3–5 executives. You map their portfolio live across Optimize / Grow / Renew. No advance data collection required — the map is built collaboratively in the room.
Deliverable
A single visual: their portfolio distribution vs. industry benchmark, plus a "Reallocation Opportunity" framing showing what a 10% capital shift would unlock in both protection and creation of capital.
What It Costs Them
Half-day of executive team time. No pre-work required. Price: $8K–$15K or included as part of a relationship-building engagement at your discretion.
Why It Works

The mechanism here is visual self-discovery. When executives build the portfolio map themselves and then see the imbalance rendered against a benchmark, the dissatisfaction is self-generated. You haven't told them they have a problem. They've shown themselves.

This removes identity threat almost entirely. The finding comes from them, not from an external critique of their strategy. The 10% reallocation simulation makes the path forward feel manageable.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"This session gives you the portfolio-level view. But portfolio imbalance is a symptom. The Diagnostic tells you where the exposure actually lives inside each bucket — which initiatives are creating serious capital and which are consuming it without a clear path to return."

S03Service EntryPrimary Buyer: Executive with AI Portfolio / AI Transformation Lead

AI Capital Exposure Review

"You're spending heavily on AI. Do you know which investments are building capital — and which are just consuming it?"

Entry Context
Any executive at an organization actively investing in AI. The anxiety is already present: unclear ROI, regulatory exposure, board scrutiny. You're naming what they're already feeling.
Format & Duration
2–3 week targeted review of their AI initiative portfolio. You apply Super Vital Assumption mapping to their top 3–5 AI investments and score them on capital creation potential versus exposure concentration. Requires document intake and 2–3 leadership interviews.
Deliverable
A one-page AI Capital Exposure Heatmap — board-presentable — showing each AI initiative's exposure profile, assumption concentration risk, and capital governance gaps. Plus a governance gap summary with 3–5 immediate actions.
What It Costs Them
Moderate lift: document intake, 2–3 leadership interviews, 2–3 weeks. Price: $15K–$25K. Scoped tightly enough to produce a boardroom-ready output that justifies the investment immediately.
Why It Works

This is your fastest-to-market wedge right now. The AI governance conversation is happening at every board table. You are not creating the urgency — you are naming and structuring anxiety that already exists. That is the most powerful selling position available.

The Heatmap travels. It gets shared. It gets presented to the board. One well-designed heatmap creates conversations you didn't initiate. AI also functions as a Trojan horse: the review introduces assumption mapping and capital impact scoring without naming the full architecture.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"This review covers your AI portfolio specifically. But AI exposure rarely lives in isolation — it's almost always connected to portfolio imbalance and liberation failures elsewhere. The full Diagnostic maps the entire capital exposure architecture and shows you how the AI layer interacts with your broader growth position."

S04Service EntryPrimary Buyer: Initiative Sponsor / Program Owner

Single Initiative Assumption Audit

"You already have something you're nervous about. Let us look at it."

Entry Context
Any relationship where a specific high-stakes initiative is visible and anxiety is present. They don't need to feel a systemic problem. They just need to be uncertain about one thing.
Format & Duration
Focused 2–3 week engagement on a single initiative. You apply the Super Vital Assumption framework: surface what the initiative depends on, classify assumptions by criticality and testability, and identify where capital is exposed.
Deliverable
An Assumption Risk Map: which assumptions are super vital, which are untested, the capital at risk if critical ones fail, and a recommended testing sequence that reduces exposure before additional capital is committed.
What It Costs Them
Only the initiative sponsor's access is required. Lightest lift of all service offers. Price: $10K–$18K. Easy to approve, easy to start, almost always produces findings that demand a broader conversation.
Why It Works

This offer bypasses every procurement and political barrier because it requires no organizational commitment — only one sponsor who is already nervous about something. The decision to engage is made by one person with one concern.

The conversion mechanism is almost structural: assumption audits on individual initiatives almost always surface portfolio-level patterns. When they see that what they thought was an initiative problem is actually a governance pattern, the natural response is "how widespread is this?" That question is your invitation.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"What we found in this initiative suggests a pattern that almost certainly exists across your portfolio. This isn't isolated — it's a governance signature. The Diagnostic surfaces that pattern systematically across your entire capital architecture, so you're not discovering it one initiative at a time."

S05Service EntryPrimary Buyer: CFO / Finance Leader

CFO Capital Discipline Review

"How much capital is currently exposed to initiatives that can't yet demonstrate learning progress?"

Entry Context
CFO or senior finance leader, often skeptical of innovation or transformation spend. Introduced through board relationships or a CEO who wants their CFO bought in before proceeding. Entry language is risk and governance — never innovation.
Format & Duration
3–4 week review of one innovation or transformation portfolio. You apply LFI vs. ROI framing — identifying which initiatives are being funded at ROI thresholds when they should be at LFI thresholds, and where escalation of commitment has set in.
Deliverable
A Capital Discipline Report: portfolio-level view of learning-stage vs. scale-stage capital allocation, an estimate of exposure reduction achievable through funding sequence correction, and stage-gate criteria the CFO can implement immediately.
What It Costs Them
Requires portfolio data and 2–3 finance and innovation leadership interviews. Price: $15K–$25K. Frames as governance strengthening and risk reduction — not transformation. Not innovation investment. Financial discipline.
Why It Works

CFOs don't feel the innovation problem — they feel the financial control problem. Budget creep, escalation of commitment, no clear stage-gate logic, no way to distinguish exploratory capital from deployment capital. This offer speaks their language entirely.

CFO buy-in is the most powerful internal accelerator available. When the CFO understands the framework, they become an internal champion. A CFO-entered engagement frequently converts to a full Diagnostic faster than any other entry path.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"This review addresses the funding sequencing layer. But capital exposure doesn't only live in how you fund — it lives in what you're creating, what you're protecting, and what capital is trapped in things that should have been stopped. The Diagnostic gives you the complete picture across all three exposure dimensions."

S06Service EntryPrimary Buyer: Board Member / Risk or Audit Committee

Board Governance Session

"Governing AI and innovation risk — what your board needs to know, and what questions it should be asking."

Entry Context
Board member, board committee, or a CEO preparing management for a board conversation on AI or growth governance. Often enters through speaking engagements, the book, or a peer referral. This is a force multiplier, not a revenue event.
Format & Duration
90-minute facilitated board or committee session. You present the Capital Exposure framework, walk through 2–3 industry case patterns, and close with a structured self-assessment: "what questions should this board be asking that it currently cannot answer?"
Deliverable
A written Governance Gap Summary: the three to five questions the board itself identified as currently unanswerable, and the structural reasons why. This document goes to management as a board-level inquiry — which becomes an organizational mandate.
What It Costs Them
90 minutes of board time. No data, no internal preparation. Price: $5K–$8K, or complimentary for strategic board relationships. Revenue value is low. Commercial multiplier value is high — a board mandate is worth more than any sales call with management.
Why It Works

Boards are uniquely receptive to governance framing because governance is their constitutional responsibility. You are not asking them to adopt a system — you are helping them fulfill an obligation they already have.

Board-initiated demand fundamentally changes the sales dynamic at management level. Instead of selling up through the organization, you are responding to pull from above. Deal velocity accelerates dramatically. One board session can unlock three Diagnostic conversations in a single organization.

Bridge to Diagnostic
"The questions this board has surfaced today are precisely what the Capital Exposure Diagnostic is designed to answer. It gives management the data, the framework, and the governance architecture to respond to this board with clarity. P01Product Entry · Wave 1Self-qualifying executive — cold to warm relationship

Growth Gap Signal Quiz

"Five minutes. A score. And a pattern you'll recognize."

Entry Mode
An executive has encountered the Growth Gap narrative and wants to know if it applies to them. They are self-qualifying before any conversation. This is the outermost ring of the diagnostic system — captures attention and generates pre-qualified pipeline before any human contact.
The Instrument
An 8–12 question digital quiz — free, ungated, shareable. Questions probe initiative portfolio patterns, capital decisions, and growth durability signals. Produces a Growth Gap Score (1–10) with a two-paragraph pattern interpretation. Completable in under five minutes.
What It Produces
A Growth Gap Score with a brief interpretation: where the organization likely sits on the Growth Gap curve and which portfolio imbalance pattern is most probable given their answers. It names the pattern. It does not resolve it.
Why It Converts
The score creates mild dissatisfaction by design. The finding is real but incomplete — it signals a problem without resolving it. The natural next question is: "Is this actually true for us?" That question is the Diagnostic. Moderate/high scores arrive conversation-ready.
Bridge to Service Entry
"You answered in a pattern that shows up in organizations facing a Growth Gap. The quiz gives you the signal. The Capital Exposure Briefing (S01) gives you the hypothesis about where specifically it's concentrated — built from your industry, your public posture, and what we typically find in organizations at your growth stage."

Capital Clarity Canvas

"A structured 20-minute self-reflection that produces an exposure map in your own words."

They encountered SolveNext through a talk or peer referral but have not yet entered a service conversation. Also functions as a pre-read that pre-populates the team's entry context before S01.

A downloadable or interactive single-page framework guiding an executive through five structured capital exposure questions — calibrated to surface exposure, not just description. Completable in 20 minutes, alone, without a facilitator. Design challenge, not an engineering challenge.

Why It Converts
No finding is harder to dismiss than one you produced yourself. The executive who completes the Canvas arrives at S01 already believing the problem exists and having named it. Conversation quality and conversion rate both rise when this precedes the service engagement.

P03Product Entry · Wave 1Executive with significant AI investment — board pressure building

AI Capital Exposure Calculator

"Five inputs. An index. And a score your board will eventually ask you about."

Entry Mode
The organization has committed meaningful capital to AI — and someone at the top is beginning to wonder whether that capital is well-sequenced. This is the product companion to S03. It qualifies the prospect and pre-populates the entry hypothesis before the service review begins.
The Instrument
A short digital form — five to seven inputs — where a leader enters their AI investment total, number of active AI initiatives, distribution across optimization vs. transformation vs. exploration, and current stage-gating discipline. Generates an AI Capital Exposure Index (Low / Moderate / High / Acute) in under three minutes.
What It Produces
An AI Capital Exposure Index with brief narrative covering: concentration of AI capital in low-uncertainty vs. high-uncertainty use cases, estimated stage-gating maturity, and primary exposure risk signal. Directional, not precise. Tells them whether their exposure warrants closer examination.
Why It Converts
Most organizations have not characterized their AI exposure in a structured way. The calculator gives them one — and reveals that their current posture has not been examined that way before. A "High" or "Acute" score is visceral. Even "Moderate" surfaces at least one concentration risk the executive wants to resolve.
Bridge to Service Entry

P04Product Entry · Wave 1Innovation-fatigued leader — frustrated by initiative failure patterns

Assumption Blindspot Identifier

"A structured tool that shows where capital is committed ahead of assumption validation."

Entry Mode
A leader who has been living with a portfolio of initiatives long enough to feel which ones are not moving. Entry trigger: a portfolio review, a failed initiative post-mortem, or frustration with the gap between initiative investment and outcomes. They want a framework that explains why it's hard — not a verdict on what they've done wrong.
The Instrument
A 6–10 question digital tool where a leader identifies three to five current strategic initiatives and answers structured questions about the key assumptions behind each — when those assumptions were last tested, and what would have to be true for the capital commitment to be justified. Takes 10–15 minutes.
What It Produces
An Assumption Concentration Risk Report: which initiatives carry the highest unvalidated assumption load relative to capital committed, the portfolio-level concentration of untested assumptions, and the top three "assumption blind spots" by capital weight.
Why It Converts
Most leaders have not previously mapped assumptions against capital commitments — completing the exercise is itself the finding. It produces a specific named problem: "We have concentrated capital in initiatives whose critical assumptions have not been tested." That problem has an obvious solution path — S04, then the Diagnostic.
Bridge to Service Entry
"The report shows where capital is most exposed to unvalidated assumptions — but it doesn't tell you which to test first or how to use testing to discipline future funding decisions. The Single Initiative Assumption Audit (S04) takes your highest-risk initiatives and builds a structured assumption-testing plan connected directly to your funding gates."

"An interactive tool that shows whether your capital distribution matches your declared strategy."

Seeing one's own allocation displayed against a growth benchmark produces self-generated dissatisfaction. The visual does what no slide deck could.

P07Product Entry · Wave 2◆ Non-ObviousAny prospect — requires no self-reporting

External Exposure Mirror

"This is what your capital posture looks like from the outside. It does not require your help to see it."

Entry Mode
An executive enters their company name or ticker. Within minutes they receive an AI-generated provisional capital health picture derived entirely from public signals — 10-K disclosures, earnings transcripts, R&D intensity, buyback patterns, hiring velocity, media sentiment, regulatory mentions. No self-reporting required.
The Instrument
An AI tool using the same public-data analysis infrastructure planned for platform onboarding. Processes external signals into a provisional capital exposure picture across extraction intensity, governance structure indicators, reputational trajectory, and leading capital health proxies. Generates a Provisional External Exposure Profile.
What It Produces
A profile showing what an outside observer would conclude about the organization's capital posture — not what the organization believes about itself. The finding answers two viscerally uncomfortable questions: "Is this what our board sees?" and "Is this what a potential acquirer sees?"
Why It Converts
Every other entry asks the executive what they think about their organization. This one tells them what an outside observer would conclude. Fundamentally harder to dismiss. The executive can't edit away a finding built from public data — which means the discomfort is more durable and demand for internal clarity more immediate.
Architectural Note

This uses the same AI public-data analysis module described in the platform architecture for generating provisional starter baselines during Diagnostic onboarding. Building the Mirror as a public-facing entry experience builds that infrastructure simultaneously — not a duplication of investment, but the same investment serving two purposes.

Bridge to Service Entry
"This is based entirely on external signals. The Diagnostic maps your internal capital architecture — the initiative-level decisions, governance patterns, and allocation choices that are producing the external picture you just saw. Most organizations find that the internal reality is more complex and more actionable than what the public signals suggest."

P08Product Entry · Wave 2◆ Non-ObviousResource-constrained executive — feeling limited, not exposed

Capital Liberation Calculator

"What could you fund if you stopped what you should stop?"

Entry Mode
The CEO who is not feeling exposed — who is feeling constrained. Not enough capital to fund what matters. Every other entry approaches capital exposure through risk framing. This one approaches it through opportunity framing — and reaches a buyer the other entries miss entirely.
The Instrument
A structured input tool where a leader identifies three to five current initiatives they suspect may be past their useful life, or answers questions about their portfolio's stop-decision history over the past 24 months. Generates an estimated Liberation Opportunity — capital that might be reclaimable and redirectable.
What It Produces
A Liberation Opportunity Estimate showing: the capital that might be freed from zombie initiatives, the governance barrier preventing the stop decision, and what that capital could accomplish if redeployed. It reframes the resource constraint as a governance problem — which is a solvable problem.
Why It Converts
Liberation is one of the five diagnostic domains, but no other entry leads with it. The constrained CEO who discovers their resource problem is actually a governance problem experiences frustrated possibility: "We have the capital. We just can't get to it." That is a mandate-creating finding.
Bridge to Service Entry
"The calculator shows what might be freed. The Diagnostic shows exactly where it's trapped — which initiatives, which governance patterns, which stop-decision failures — and how to structure the redeployment so the liberated capital actually reaches the things you want to fund."

Governor Identity Assessment

"Where do you sit on the capital governance maturity spectrum — and what does Stage 4 look like?"

Entry Mode
A significant portion of enterprise buyers make decisions with a personal identity dimension. This entry operates at the individual leader level — an angle no other entry in this portfolio addresses. Triggers: thought leadership encounters, professional development conversations, peer comparisons.

A short individual assessment — 12–15 questions — placing the executive on the Instigator → Steward → Governor → Architect capital governance maturity spectrum. Returns a Governance Maturity Stage (1–4) with description, behavioral gap narrative, and a picture of what Stage 4 looks like.
What It Produces
A personal governance maturity stage with a description of what Stage 4 looks like and what specific behavioral gaps exist. The finding is personal, not institutional. That is a qualitatively different kind of discomfort to resolve — and a qualitatively different kind of finding to share.
Why It Converts
This is the entry with the highest viral coefficient. Identity-anchored findings are shareable in ways that organizational vulnerability findings are not. One result creates three conversations. It also creates simultaneous demand signals: individual demand for the certification pathway and organizational demand for the Diagnostic.
Bridge to Service Entry and Certification
"The assessment shows where you sit as an individual capital governor. The Diagnostic shows what it means for your organization's portfolio — and whether the governance maturity of your leadership team is matched to the exposure level your capital architecture has created."

Think Wrong → Diagnostic Bridge

"A facilitation protocol that runs at the close of every Think Wrong session — and converts the room into a Diagnostic pipeline."

One central question the room cannot currently answer. The finding comes from their session, not from a SolveNext pitch.
Why It Converts

It also elevates the conversation to executive level — correcting the documented problem of Think Wrong engagements landing with mid-level buyers who lack Diagnostic mandate. Build time: one to two weeks. No engineering required.
Implementation Note

This is a facilitation protocol, not an engineering challenge. The investment is in designing the reframe questions and the one-page Capital Exposure Signal template. Every Think Wrong engagement becomes a Diagnostic pipeline event from the moment this protocol is deployed — with no change to the Think Wrong offer itself and no visible commercial motion for the client.

Every entry — service or product — has one job: produce a finding the client wants to resolve, and make the Capital Exposure Diagnostic the obvious next step.

The service entries work when you have a relationship. The product entries work when you don't — or when the executive prefers to discover the problem themselves. Together they ensure no prospect is too cold, no contact too early, and no executive too self-sufficient to find their way into the Diagnostic.

The four entries marked ◆ are architecturally non-obvious. They reach buyers the other entries do not: the skeptic, the constrained CEO, the career-focused individual, and the Think Wrong client who has never been given a path to the governance conversation.
