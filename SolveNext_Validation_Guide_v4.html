The Governing Principle
Test the
right assumptions
in the right order.

These three conversations are not sales calls. They are structured assumption tests. The SVA Drill tells us which assumptions to test first — the ones that are low certainty and high impact. Lean Customer Development tells us how — through stories rooted in past behavior, not opinions about the future.

W

Wanted. Does the market feel this pain acutely enough to fund action? Is the CEO/CFO mandate tension a real, named governance problem — or an intellectual recognition?

D

Doable. Can we deliver the promise reliably — without Greg in every session, across sectors, producing results a CFO would present to a board without qualification?

W

Worth It. Is the business case viable for client and for Solve Next? Will $125K+ move through procurement at mid-enterprise — $150K+ at large enterprise? Does the diagnostic convert to retainers at a rate that funds the architecture?

"Assumptions in the Tackle First quadrant are the ones that can make or break the opportunity. They have high impact on the outcome — and we don't yet know if they're true. Test those first."

Pre-Call Preparation — 15 Minutes
Know before
you dial.

15 minutes of preparation changes the quality of assumption signal you receive. Complete all three steps and write the hypothesis card before every conversation.

Step 0 — Check Pre-Conversation Activation Status

Two entry instruments now activate prospects before any human conversation begins. A contact who has already used either arrives partially activated — the Wanted assumption may already be moving. Check both before you dial.

Capital Sentinel (P07 · Live)

A free AI scan of any named organization — generates a Growth Gap Curve, six-domain status assessment (Human, Intellectual, Reputational, Political, Social, Financial), and a live alert feed. No self-reporting required.

If this contact has run a scan: Which domains are at Watch or Alert? What is the Growth Gap index? Use as a story prompt: "I noticed from the Sentinel scan that your [domain] domain showed a Watch signal — walk me through what's been happening in that area." The Wanted assumption may already be partially confirmed.

If they haven't: Run a scan on their organization yourself before the call. Use it as a preparation tool — the domain signals it surfaces become your story prompts.

Growth Gap Phase Assessment (P01 · Building)

A self-directed tool that produces a Phase I / II / III cascade determination — where the organization sits in the Growth Gap progression. Ten questions, four minutes. Designed to create self-generated urgency before any conversation begins.

If this contact has completed the assessment: Which phase did they score? Phase II and III are your highest-urgency conversations — the Wanted assumption is already confirmed. Open with: "You scored Phase [II/III] — which of the signals it flagged was most recognizable to you?"

If they haven't: Send the Phase Assessment as a pre-call warm-up tool — it activates before you speak, and arrives as a finding, not a pitch.

Step 2 — 10-minute scan for assumption-relevant signals

Wanted signal
Any visible governance stress?

Restructuring, leadership turnover, analyst downgrades, or regulatory inquiry in the past 18 months. Each is a story prompt for a specific Tackle First Wanted assumption. "I noticed X — walk me through the governance conversation around that."

Doable signal
Any prior governance frameworks in place?

Big Four governance engagement, internal task force, prior diagnostic output from another firm. If yes: they have already tried to solve a related problem. What did that produce? This tests whether our methodology offers something the prior attempt didn't.

Worth It signal
Any visible capital allocation decisions?

Major acquisition, transformation program, or capital raise in the last 12 months. These suggest budget authority exists and capital allocation decisions are live. A contact who has recently made a $50M+ decision is a more credible worth-it test than one who hasn't.

All three
Strong financials + upstream erosion?

The highest-value profile: financially strong company whose non-financial capital is quietly deteriorating. Tests all three dimensions simultaneously — the pain is real (Wanted), our detection method adds something financial reporting doesn't (Doable), and the cost of not acting is quantifiable (Worth It).

Step 3 — Set your signal threshold before you dial

Strong signal

The contact tells a specific story, names a capital domain and a decision, has already tried to solve the problem with imperfect results. At least one Tackle First assumption moves from Low to High certainty. The hypothesis is confirmed or interestingly wrong.

Partial signal

Recognition is genuine but no past behavior or story surfaces. The contact speaks in generalities. Tackle First assumptions remain at Low Certainty. The hypothesis is neither confirmed nor disconfirmed. Re-test in 90 days.

Not signal

"That sounds interesting." Future-tense responses. No story. No past behavior. No evidence the pain has been felt acutely enough to produce action. No Tackle First assumption moves. Hypothesis cannot be evaluated. Move on.

Part 1 — Opening the Conversation — Minutes 0–5
Open with
the tension.
Ask for the story.

Do not open with the framework. Open with the tension — and immediately ask for a story. Stories are falsifiable. Opinions are polite. You need stories to move Tackle First assumptions off Low Certainty.

Version A — Warm client or current contact

"I've been thinking about something we touched on in [context of prior work]. The organizations I'm talking to right now are all navigating the same structural tension — the pressure to grow aggressively and the pressure to protect the cost of that growth. The CFO and the CEO are both right, but most governance systems only give them one set of instruments. Can you tell me about a recent moment when you felt that tension most acutely — a specific decision where the financial data wasn't giving you the full picture?" Testing: WANTED — CFOs feel the mandate tension as a specific, funded governance problem.

Version B — New contact or referral

"The work I'm focused on right now is about a specific governance problem. Most organizations are trying to govern growth with instruments designed for financial capital — but the things that actually determine whether growth is durable sit in human capital, intellectual capital, and a few other domains that financial reporting doesn't reach. I'd love to start by hearing about a time when that gap was visible in your organization — a specific decision or situation where you knew something important wasn't in the numbers. Does anything come to mind?" Testing: WANTED — Clients connect their lived governance experience to the cascade narrative unprompted.
Ask one question, then go fully quiet. The story they reach for is the signal. The silence is working. Every sentence you speak is a sentence they are not speaking.

In terms of the decision itself, or the confidence with which you made it. This is the Worth It question in story form. Listen for whether they respond with a number, a decision, or a governance improvement — all three are signal. A contact who says "I would have approved the investment with confidence instead of deferring it" has just described the diagnostic's value proposition in their own words. Worth It — moves assumption: the pain is acute enough to authorize a $125K+ diagnostic within a normal procurement cycle (mid-enterprise)
A5
Disconfirming — What would have to change for this to not be a problem at all? Under what conditions would you feel like you had this fully solved? Tests whether the pain is structural or situational. If they easily describe a condition that resolves it, the pain may not be deep enough to fund a diagnostic. If they struggle to answer, the pain is structural — and a Tackle First Wanted assumption moves toward High Certainty.

B1
Tell me about a departure in the last 18 months that had a bigger impact on the organization than the official announcement suggested. What happened to the work that person was carrying? → "How long did it take to feel that?" / "Has it been replaced — or absorbed?" / "Where did that knowledge go?" Wanted — surfaces the human capital erosion that precedes financial impact by 12–18 months
B2

B3

If they struggle to answer, visibility is not the real problem. If they immediately name three things, the governance gap is real. A Tackle First Wanted assumption moves toward High Certainty.
Module C — Intellectual Capital · Ask if A surfaces technology or systems signals · Tests Wanted + Doable assumptions
C1
Walk me through the last time a technology modernization or systems investment came up for decision and was deferred. What happened in that conversation — and what has happened since? → "How many cycles has it been deferred?" / "What's the cost of that deferral now compared to when it first came up?" Wanted — three or more consecutive deferrals is a Stage 1–2 signal and confirms the cascade narrative
C2
Where has your organization tried to protect or build intellectual capital in the last two years — and what happened? Did it work the way you expected? → "What made it harder than anticipated?" / "What would you do differently?" / "Is that effort still active?" Doable — what they tried and failed at defines exactly what our methodology needs to offer that existing approaches cannot
C3
Disconfirming — If the deferred modernizations were completed and your core systems were current — what specifically becomes possible that isn't possible now? Tests whether intellectual capital erosion is creating real strategic constraints (Tackle First signal) or just operational friction (lower urgency). Strategic constraints are what the diagnostic is designed to surface.
Module D — Portfolio Allocation · Ask if A surfaces investment drift · Tests Wanted + Worth It assumptions
D1
Tell me about the last time someone in your organization raised the gap between your declared strategic priorities and where the capital was actually going. What happened in that conversation? → "Who raised it?" / "How was it received?" / "Did anything change as a result?" Wanted — the story of what happens when portfolio drift is named reveals the governance detection gap in action
D2
What have you tried in order to stop or exit an initiative that you knew was consuming more than it was building? What happened when you tried? → "Why was it hard to stop?" / "Who pushed back, and what was their argument?" / "Is it still running?" Doable — what prevents liberation is the governance problem. Whether we can solve what they've already tried tells us whether our methodology is Doable for this client profile.
D3
If you could quantify the cost of that portfolio drift — the capital consumed by initiatives that weren't building toward your declared priorities — what would be a rough order of magnitude? And what would it have been worth to surface that earlier? This is the Worth It question for portfolio misalignment. A CFO who can put a number on portfolio drift — even a rough one — has just made the diagnostic investment case themselves. Record the exact number or range they name. Worth It — moves assumption: the diagnostic investment is proportionate to the cost of the problem it surfaces
Module E — Financial Capital & the Cascade · Use with CFOs and board members · Tests Worth It Tackle First assumptions
E1
Think about the last time your organization's financial performance looked strong but something upstream felt fragile — talent, systems, reputation, something. Did you act on that instinct? What happened? → "What made you trust or distrust that instinct?" / "In hindsight, was the instinct right?" / "What would you have needed to act on it with confidence?" Wanted — the story of an instinct that was right but couldn't be acted on for lack of data is the diagnostic value proposition in their own words
E2
Walk me through the last governance decision you made where you felt you had less information than you needed. What was the decision, and what did you do with the uncertainty? → "How did that decision turn out?" / "What information, specifically, would have changed it?" Worth It — the specific information gap they name is the diagnostic's deliverable. How much was that decision worth? What would better information have been worth?
E3
Disconfirming — If you had real-time visibility into non-financial capital exposure — if you could see human, intellectual, and political erosion before it reached the financials — what governance decisions would you make differently? Are there any you wouldn't change at all? The "wouldn't change at all" clause is the disconfirming pressure. If they say everything would change, ask: "What's been in the way of building that visibility?" The answer tests whether our approach is Doable or whether there's a structural barrier we haven't accounted for.

Appendix — Handling Common Responses
What to say
when they push back.

Handle objections without defensiveness. Where possible, respond with a question that returns to past behavior — and that simultaneously tests a Tackle First assumption. The goal is not to win the debate. It is to keep the assumption test alive.

01
"We already have McKinsey / Deloitte / EY advising us on governance." "Those firms are very capable. The difference is the instrument set — a governance engagement anchored in financial metrics can only surface exposure that your financial system is already measuring. The diagnostic surfaces what financial reporting cannot see." → "When your existing advisors last assessed governance risk — which of the six capital domains was in their dataset? Has there been a situation where you felt their advice was limited by the data they had?" Testing: DOABLE — our methodology offers something prior approaches cannot.
02
"We have good governance processes." "Can I ask — when your governance processes last flagged a concern in human capital or intellectual capital specifically, before it showed up in the financials, what did that look like? What was the mechanism?" If they cannot describe a specific instance, the Wanted assumption is confirmed and the Doable assumption is neutral. If they describe a working mechanism, you learn what already exists — and what it misses.
03
"What does this cost?" "Before I give you the investment, let me describe what you receive — because the price makes more sense in context." [Describe five diagnostic outputs: Stage determination, evidence quality audit, cascade pathway analysis, entropy force assessment, board-ready governance briefing.] "The investment starts at $125,000 for a mid-enterprise organization, $150,000 for a large enterprise, and $175–200,000 for complex or regulated sectors. For context: a one-notch credit downgrade on a $10 billion debt position costs $75–$150 million annually." Worth It assumption test: if they respond to the $75–$150M figure with recognition ("that's the kind of risk we're managing"), the worth-it assumption is moving toward High Certainty. If they ask for a smaller scope, it may be in Monitor Periodically for this contact today.
04
"We are not ready for something like this right now." "Understood. Can I ask what's made the timing difficult? Has there been a moment recently when the timing felt closer — what was happening then?" → Return to the story of when the problem was most acute. This tests the Wanted assumption: is the pain structural and persistent, or situational and resolved? The answer tells us whether this assumption is Low or High Certainty for this profile.
05
"How is this different from what we already measure?" "Which of the five non-financial capitals — human, intellectual, political, reputational, social — does your current system give you a leading indicator for? Can you describe what that looks like for human capital specifically?" If they describe a specific mechanism: you learn what exists and what it misses.
