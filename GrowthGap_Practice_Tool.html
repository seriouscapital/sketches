Growth Gap - Whiteboard Practice Tool
EP 01The Provocation — The Growth Gap Curve“Your best quarter might be your most dangerous one”
“The quarter you are most proud of may be the one a historian writes about.”
SETUP: Clean whiteboard. Nothing pre-drawn. Start with the time axis, then build both curves live so the gap reveals itself. The visual argument lands only if the audience sees the divergence emerge in real time.
STEP 1
Draw a horizontal TIME axis across the bottom. Draw a vertical PERFORMANCE axis on the left.
STEP 2
Draw the FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE curve rising from left to right — smooth, confident, upward. Label it at the right end.
STEP 3
Below it, draw the ENDURANCE curve — starting roughly parallel, then slowly declining. Use dashed line. Label it MULTI-CAPITAL ENDURANCE.
STEP 4
Shade the GAP between the two curves. Write 'THE GROWTH GAP' in the shaded region.
STEP 5
Draw a vertical dashed line at the peak of the financial curve. Label it 'TRIGGER EVENT.'
STEP 6
Write 'FINANCIAL CAPITAL FAILS LAST' above the diagram. Draw an arrow pointing to the right end of the financial curve.
STEP 7
Write the Boeing dates: '2013-2018: FINANCIAL CURVE RISING. 2014-2018: ENDURANCE DECLINING.' Draw two diverging lines to illustrate.
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'Which of your six capitals would you have to guess if you had to score them today?'
EP 02The System — The Capital Cascade“The metrics that got you here are hiding what will end you”
“You are flying a plane using only the fuel gauge. The other instruments are not optional.”
SETUP: Build the cascade top to bottom. Each capital is a band — draw them in sequence, then add the arrows. The directional flow is the argument: financial capital is at the bottom, not the top.
STEP 1
Write 'SIX CAPITALS' at the top. Draw six boxes in a column below: HUMAN, INTELLECTUAL, SOCIAL, REPUTATIONAL, POLITICAL, FINANCIAL.
STEP 2
Draw arrows flowing downward between each box — from HUMAN to INTELLECTUAL to SOCIAL to REPUTATIONAL to POLITICAL to FINANCIAL. Label 'THE CASCADE.'
STEP 3
Highlight the top 5 boxes in BURGUNDY/RED. Highlight FINANCIAL at the bottom in INDIGO/BLUE. Write 'LAGGING INDICATOR' next to FINANCIAL.
STEP 4
Write 'TIMING ASYMMETRY' to the right. Draw a timeline showing ENDURANCE DECLINING starting early, FINANCIAL DECLINING starting later.
STEP 5
Draw a second cascade diagram labeled 'CREDIT SUISSE.' Show REPUTATIONAL failing first, then SOCIAL (client withdrawals), then POLITICAL (regulatory), then FINANCIAL.
STEP 6
Write 'INTERDEPENDENCE' and draw bidirectional arrows between several boxes, showing that healthy capitals reinforce each other.
STEP 7
Write the key question: 'WHICH CAPITAL IS MOST UNDERWEIGHTED AT YOUR COMPANY?' Draw a small assessment grid with the 6 capitals and scoring.
STEP 8
Write the cascade summary: 'Human → Intellectual → Social → Reputational → Political → Financial.' Circle FINANCIAL. Write 'OUTPUT LAYER — NOT ORIGIN.'
EP 03The Trap — Capital Distortion vs. Symmetry“Nobody at Boeing was trying to destroy Boeing”
“The system was working exactly as designed. That is the problem.”
SETUP: Start with the compensation math — one large bar and five tiny ones. Build escalation without malice as a sequence. Then reveal the contrast state: six equal bars. The emotional arc runs from recognition to anger to possibility.
STEP 1
Draw two columns. Left: 'FINANCIAL CAPITAL — ~90% of governance attention.' Right: 'ALL OTHER CAPITALS — ~10%.' Show dramatically unequal sizing.
STEP 2
Write 'WHAT THE INCENTIVE SYSTEM IS ACTUALLY SAYING: Financial capital is what governs here.' Draw an arrow from the compensation math to a behavioral response.
STEP 3
Write 'ESCALATION WITHOUT MALICE.' Below it, draw a sequence: RATIONAL DECISION → RATIONAL DECISION → RATIONAL DECISION → FRAGILITY. Each decision has a check mark.
STEP 4
Write 'CULTURE = BEHAVIORAL SHADOW OF CAPITAL WEIGHTING.' Underline it.
STEP 5
Draw the SNOWMELT diagram — a circle representing the enterprise, FINANCIAL at the center, five capitals at the edges with melt arrows pointing inward.
STEP 6
Draw the contrast: LEFT panel shows FINANCIAL dominant bar with five tiny absent bars. Label 'CAPITAL DISTORTION.' RIGHT panel shows SIX EQUAL BARS. Label 'CAPITAL SYMMETRY.'
STEP 7
Write the five most common rewarded-fragility decisions: 1. Cut training in a hard quarter. 2. Reduce supplier margins. 3. Ship early. 4. Defer culture. 5. Manage regs to minimum.
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'Which decisions at your organization were escalation without malice? What was the governance signal that should have been visible?'
EP 04The Solution — Capital Symmetry Governance“The architecture your board does not know it is missing”
“The board approved everything. The company failed anyway. That is not a board problem. It is a governance architecture problem.”
SETUP: Build top to bottom: three levels, then add the two roles on the sides, then the three verbs. The diagram should feel like an operating system being assembled live. End with the full system visible.
STEP 1
Draw three stacked rectangles labeled (top to bottom): 'ENTERPRISE GOVERNANCE — Board', 'PORTFOLIO ALLOCATION — Executive Team', 'INITIATIVE DEVELOPMENT — Operational Leaders'. Draw downward arrows between them.
STEP 2
On the left side of the stack, write 'THE ARCHITECT.' On the right side, write 'THE GOVERNOR.' Draw brackets connecting them to all three levels.
STEP 3
Write the three governing verbs large: 'CREATE PROTECT LIBERATE.' Below each, give an example decision.
STEP 4
Draw a spectrum from 'REPORTING' to 'GOVERNING.' Place most current boards near REPORTING. Describe the difference.
STEP 5
Write the governance test: For each capital: 1. Do we have a threshold? 2. Do we have a decision right? 3. Do we have a CEO accountability mechanism?
STEP 6
Draw the BOARD PACK diagnostic. Show 80% of pages as financial, 20% as everything else. Write 'THIS IS NOT A DELIBERATE DESIGN. IT IS A DEFAULT.'
STEP 7
Write 'Serious Capital Architecture' at top. Draw the full CSG model compactly: three levels + two roles + three verbs + six capitals.
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'Do we govern our capital system — or do we report on it? What would need to be true for the answer to change?'
EP 05The Evidence — GE vs. Microsoft Recovery Curves“What Satya Nadella saw that Jeff Immelt did not”
“One CEO restored the capital system before the crisis arrived. One did not. The difference was not intelligence. It was what they chose to measure.”
SETUP: Two diagrams side by side from the start. Build GE first — the financial rise, the endurance decline, the trigger. Then build Microsoft — the Nadella marker, endurance recovering first, financial following. The contrast is the proof.
STEP 1
Draw two side-by-side charts. LEFT: 'GE' with financial performance rising then collapsing. RIGHT: 'MICROSOFT' with a dip then a strong recovery. Label both axes TIME and PERFORMANCE.
STEP 2
On the GE chart, draw the ENDURANCE curve (dashed) declining years before the FINANCIAL curve. Mark the trigger event. Write 'GOVERNANCE SAW THE LAGGING SIGNAL.'
STEP 3
Write the GE cascade sequence: 'Intellectual capital (strategy incoherence) → Human capital (organizational strain) → Reputational capital (investor trust) → Financial capital (rupture).' Show timing gaps between each.
STEP 4
On the MICROSOFT chart, draw the ENDURANCE curve recovering BEFORE the financial curve rises. Mark 2014 (Nadella) clearly. Write 'ENDURANCE LEADS FINANCIAL BY ~18 MONTHS.'
STEP 5
Write the MICROSOFT capital sequence: '2014: Human capital rebalanced → 2015: Intellectual capital rebuilt → 2016: Social capital restored → 2017-18: Reputational capital recovered → 2018+: Financial performance accelerates.'
STEP 6
Write 'HOW DID NADELLA KNOW IT WAS WORKING?' Below: 'The best engineers are choosing to work here. Developers are building in our ecosystem. Enterprise customers are calling us first.'
STEP 7
Write the leadership contrast: 'NOT ABOUT INTELLIGENCE OR INTENT. ABOUT WHAT THE GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE WAS DESIGNED TO SEE.'
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'If your governance architecture can only see financial capital, what leading signals are you currently missing that you will wish you had seen two years from now?'
EP 06The Practice — The Rebalancing Cycle“The first rebalance attempt almost always fails”
“The hardest governance conversation a CEO will ever have is not with the board. It is with the incentive system.”
SETUP: Draw the first attempt as a line that rises then falls back — show the reversion clearly. Then build the four structural conditions. Then draw the cycle that makes it durable. Honest about difficulty; clear about the path.
STEP 1
Draw a timeline. At the start, write 'GOVERNANCE INITIATIVE LAUNCHED.' Show a line rising. Then mark '6-12 MONTHS' and show the line falling back to baseline. Label 'FIRST ATTEMPT: REVERSION.'
STEP 2
Write 'INCENTIVE GRAVITY' large. Draw an arrow pulling the governance initiative back down toward financial optimization. Show it as a constant force.
STEP 3
Write the three reasons first attempts fail: '1. Aspirational targets without breach thresholds. 2. Guidelines without decision rights. 3. Visibility without accountability.'
STEP 4
Draw the FOUR CONDITIONS for a durable second attempt: 1. THRESHOLDS WITH TEETH. 2. FORMAL DECISION RIGHTS. 3. CEO-LEVEL ACCOUNTABILITY. 4. GOVERNANCE CADENCE.
STEP 5
Write 'OPTIONALITY AS GOVERNANCE MUSCLE.' Draw a leader with multiple paths available vs. a leader with only one path (forced choice under pressure).
STEP 6
Draw the GOVERNANCE CYCLE: MEASURE → THRESHOLD BREACH → DECISION PROTOCOL → REBALANCING ACTION → MEASURE. Show it as a repeating loop.
STEP 7
Write 'FROM EVENT TO HABIT.' Show a graph of governance consistency over time, with consistent governance outperforming sporadic governance on endurance.
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'Has your organization tried to rebalance before? What pulled it back? Which of the four structural conditions was missing?'
EP 07The Acceleration — AI Compression Diagram“AI is not creating asymmetry. It is compressing it.”
“AI does not introduce a new risk. It removes the lag time that used to give you a chance to catch it.”
SETUP: Start with the pre-AI timeline (slow divergence), then draw the compressed AI timeline. The visual shock of compression is the argument. Add the three mechanisms last as annotation.
STEP 1
Draw two timelines side by side. LEFT: 'PRE-AI: 5-7 year detection window.' RIGHT: 'AI-ACCELERATED: 18-24 month detection window.' Show the compression visually.
STEP 2
Write 'THREE ACCELERATION MECHANISMS.' Number them 1, 2, 3 with space below each.
STEP 3
Write '1. FASTER EXTRACTION.' Draw an arrow showing endurance capital converting to financial capital at speed. Label 'AI-ENABLED EFFICIENCY.'
STEP 4
Write '2. ILLUSION OF PRECISION.' Draw a dashboard that looks comprehensive but only covers financial/operational signals. Mark the non-financial capitals as dark/absent.
STEP 5
Write '3. NEW CAPITAL RISKS.' Below: 'Human: displacement, capability loss. Social: trust fracture, community damage. Political: regulatory exposure in every jurisdiction.'
STEP 6
Write 'THE UPSIDE: AI COMPRESSES RECOVERY TOO.' Draw the same timelines, but now showing a governed enterprise detecting and recovering faster with AI.
STEP 7
Write 'EVERY AI INVESTMENT IS A CAPITAL ALLOCATION DECISION.' Draw a grid: AI DEPLOYMENT TYPE vs. CAPITAL CREATED vs. CAPITAL AT RISK.
STEP 8
Write the closing question: 'For your most significant AI investment, what capital does it create across all six dimensions? What capital does it put at risk?'
EP 08The Long View — Long-Horizon Divergence“Capital symmetry is not a destination. It is how endurance compounds.”
“The enterprises that govern all six capitals do not just survive disruption. They become harder to disrupt over time.”
SETUP: Ten-year timeline. Two lines starting together, then diverging with increasing separation. Build the three compounding mechanisms as annotation. The closing diagram should feel like a promise, not just a prediction.
STEP 1
Draw a long horizontal timeline — 10 YEARS. Draw two curves starting together: one for a 'GOVERNED ENTERPRISE' (all six capitals) rising steadily, one for an 'UNGOVERNED ENTERPRISE' (financial capital only) rising initially then plateauing or declining.
STEP 2
Mark the DIVERGENCE POINT at approximately year 3-4. Write 'THE GAP DOES NOT WIDEN GRADUALLY. IT COMPOUNDS.'
STEP 3
Write '3 COMPOUNDING MECHANISMS.' Number them. First: 'TRUST RESERVOIR ACCUMULATION.'
STEP 4
Second mechanism: 'TALENT MAGNETISM.' Draw an arrow showing the best talent migrating toward governed enterprises over time.
STEP 5
Third mechanism: 'GOVERNANCE OPTIONALITY.' Draw a leader with multiple strategic paths available vs. a leader forced into a single path under pressure.
STEP 6
Write 'SYMMETRY IS NOT BALANCE. IT IS ALIGNMENT.' Explain: balance = equal investment. Alignment = investment proportional to strategic priorities and endurance requirements.
STEP 7
Draw the ENDURANCE DEFINITION: 'The capacity to sustain performance across cycles of disruption.' Show a graph with multiple disruption cycles — the governed enterprise navigating each one; the ungoverned one failing at one.
STEP 8
Write the closing statement: 'Capital symmetry is not a destination. It is an operating condition. The enterprise that governs completely becomes harder to disrupt over time. That is the compound return of complete governance.'
